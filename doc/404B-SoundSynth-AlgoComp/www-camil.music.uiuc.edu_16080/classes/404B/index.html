About

Music 404B is an intermediate course in software sound synthesis and algorithmic composition. Students should already have had experience with either sound synthesis or algorithmic composition, preferably by taking the preceding course "Introduction to Computer Composition". Topics covered in 404B include:

  • Real-time scheduling: working with compositional algorithms in real time: integrating algorithms with concurrently running synthesis programs like Supercollider and MAX/MSP.
  • Algorithmic notation: how to compute complex notation input into programs like Lilypond and Sibelius.
  • Spectral composition: how to derive compositional materials from FFT analysis and algorithmically manipulate spectral information to produce scores for traditional instruments.
  • Digital sound processing: applying compositional and sound processing/synthesis algorithms to generate multi-channel "tape" pieces.

Students are expected to have had some experience with either sound synthesis, real-time systems or algorithmic composition. Knowledge of Lisp syntax (either Scheme or Common Lisp) is assumed. Grades will be based on class participation and compositional projects.
